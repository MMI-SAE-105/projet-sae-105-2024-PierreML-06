glossary

Telephone:

Device used to transmit voice over

short distances. Invented by Alexander

Graham Bell in 1876, it evolved from a simple

device to complex technology including

modern smartphones.

Landline:

A communication device connected to a fixed

telephone line, primarily used before the rise

of mobile phones.

Mobile phone:

A wireless communication device

that enables calls and messaging,

making mobile communication possible.

Smartphone:

An advanced mobile phone combining

the features of a traditional phone

with those of a computer, providing

Internet access, photography, and app usage.

5G:

Fifth generation of mobile network technology,

offering ultra-fast speeds, reduced latency,

and the ability to connect a large number of

devices simultaneously.

It enables the development of connected devices

and advanced applications.

Connected objects (IoT):

Devices and objects integrating communication

technologies to interact with each other and

users via the Internet.

Psychological thriller:

A film genre that seeks to evoke

intense emotions by exploring the human

psyche and its fears.
